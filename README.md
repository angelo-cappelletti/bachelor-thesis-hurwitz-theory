# Bachelor Thesis on Hurwitz Theory

Undergraduate thesis written in LaTeX for the Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics at the university of Bologna, focusing on Hurwitz theory, Hurwitz numbers and Riemann surfaces.

## Title

**Hurwitz Theory: From Representations to Tropical Geometry**

## Abstract

> “How many maps between Riemann surfaces exist once all discrete invariants are fixed?”
>
> This question was first posed by the German mathematician Adolf Hurwitz toward the end of the 19th century, giving rise to what is now known as Hurwitz theory.
>
> In recent years, connections between Hurwitz theory and several areas of mathematics have emerged, including combinatorics, algebraic geometry, representation theory, topology, differential equations, and even physics.
>
> This thesis provides an introduction to Hurwitz theory and develops the necessary background to approach the subject.
>
> The first chapter introduces the theory of Riemann surfaces and the Riemann–Hurwitz theorem, which establishes a relation between discrete invariants and provides a necessary condition for the existence of holomorphic maps between Riemann surfaces.
>
> The second chapter focuses on Hurwitz numbers. Covering space theory and monodromy are reviewed, and the Riemann existence theorem is presented to connect topological coverings with holomorphic maps.
>
> The third chapter discusses the representation theory of finite groups, especially the symmetric group, and its relation to Hurwitz numbers through Burnside’s formula. The thesis also briefly introduces generating functions, degeneration formulas, and tropical Hurwitz theory.
